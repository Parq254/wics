Bill Summary

The Financial Reform Act of 2023 is a comprehensive piece of legislation aimed at addressing systemic issues in the financial sector, enhancing consumer protections, and preventing future economic crises. This bill represents the most significant overhaul of financial regulations since the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2010.

Current Status: In Committee

Key Provisions

  • Establishes the Office of Financial Technology Innovation to regulate and promote responsible fintech development
  • Increases capital requirements for systemically important financial institutions
  • Implements a "financial transaction tax" on high-frequency trading
  • Expands the authority of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
  • Creates new regulations for cryptocurrency exchanges and digital assets
  • Enhances whistleblower protections in the financial sector
  • Requires climate risk disclosures for public companies

Legislative Timeline

  • January 15, 2023: Bill introduced in the House of Representatives
  • February 3, 2023: Referred to the House Financial Services Committee
  • March 10, 2023: Public hearings begin
  • April 5, 2023: Amendments proposed
  • Current: Under review in committee

Projected Impact

The Congressional Budget Office estimates that the Financial Reform Act of 2023 would:

  • Generate $20 billion in revenue over 10 years through the financial transaction tax
  • Create approximately 5,000 new jobs in financial regulation and oversight
  • Potentially reduce the risk of a major financial crisis by 30% over the next two decades
  • Increase consumer savings by an estimated $40 billion through enhanced protections and reduced fees

Supporters and Opposition

Key Supporters:

Sen. Elizabeth Warren (D-MA)

"This bill provides the comprehensive reform our financial system desperately needs to protect consumers and prevent another devastating economic crisis."

Rep. Katie Porter (D-CA)

"The Financial Reform Act puts Main Street before Wall Street and ensures that our regulatory framework keeps pace with financial innovation."

Key Opposition:

Sen. Pat Toomey (R-PA)

"This bill overreaches and will stifle innovation in our financial markets, ultimately hurting American competitiveness."

U.S. Chamber of Commerce

"The proposed regulations will create unnecessary burdens on businesses and could slow economic growth."

Public Opinion

According to a recent Gallup poll:

  • 62% of Americans support stronger regulations on large financial institutions
  • 55% are in favor of the proposed financial transaction tax
  • 70% believe more needs to be done to protect consumers from predatory financial practices

Next Steps

The bill is expected to pass through committee with some amendments by late summer 2023. If approved, it will move to the House floor for a full vote, likely in early fall. Should it pass the House, the bill will then proceed to the Senate for consideration.
